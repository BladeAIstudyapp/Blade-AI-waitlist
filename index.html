Explore how Shakespeare presents Macbeth's ambition throughout the play.

In Macbeth, Shakespeare presents Macbeth's ambition as a powerful force that ultimately leads to his downfall. At the beginning of the play, Macbeth is portrayed as a brave and loyal soldier, but the witches' prophecies awaken his desire for power.

In the extract, Macbeth is already considering the possibility of becoming king. Shakespeare uses Macbeth's internal conflict to show the struggle between his ambition and his moral conscience.

As the play progresses, Macbeth's ambition becomes increasingly destructive. After murdering Duncan, he becomes consumed by paranoia and fear, leading him to commit further crimes in an attempt to secure his position.

Why I built Blade AI

Every student deserves access to high-quality feedback

As a GCSE student, I saw how access to high-quality feedback often depends on having a tutor or teacher available. I wanted to build a tool that gives every student access to instant, personalised feedback, regardless of their background.

Blade AI exists to help level the playing field and make effective studying more accessible to everyone.
